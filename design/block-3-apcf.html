4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

Three independent groups of 6 first-order all-pass stages cascaded in series per stereo channel — 18 all-pass stages total per channel. Mixed with the dry signal, they create the classic phaser comb: a sweep of notches and peaks across the frequency spectrum. With three independent groups tuned to different frequency ranges, the comb pattern spreads across the full audio band, producing rich, formant-like resonances — like a vowel filter that breathes with modulation.

  • Group 1: Low formant (~100 Hz – 1 kHz)
  • Group 2: Mid formant (~500 Hz – 5 kHz)
  • Group 3: High formant (~2 kHz – 20 kHz)

FB DIST BLEND: replaces the former 3-position SOURCE switch (Internal / Blend / Post-Dist). The continuous knob smoothly crossfades the feedback source from clean APF output (0%) to post-distortion signal (100%). All intermediate values are musically useful.

COMB BYPASS: replaces the former DRY/WET mix control. Controls the pre-comb VCA level — at 0%, the signal bypasses the comb chain entirely; at 100%, full comb processing is applied.

Phase 2 — Transfer Functions

Single 1st-order APF stage:
  H_APF(s) = (s − ω₀) / (s + ω₀)
  Magnitude: |H_APF(jω)| = 1 for all ω (unity gain — all-pass)
  Phase: φ(ω) = π − 2 arctan(ω / ω₀)

Six cascaded stages (one group):
  H_6(s) = [(s − ω₀) / (s + ω₀)]^6
  Phase: φ_6(ω) = 6 × [π − 2 arctan(ω / ω₀)]

Notch frequencies:
  ω_notch_n = ω₀ × tan((5 − 2n)π / 12)   for n = 0, 1, 2  → 3 notch pairs per group

OTA voltage control:
  ω₀ = g_m / C_apf
  g_m = I_abc / (2 × V_T)   V_T ≈ 26 mV at room temp
  1V/oct: +1V → I_abc × 2 → ω₀ × 2 → f × 2

Capacitor values:
  Group 1 (100 Hz–1 kHz):   C_apf = 1.5 nF
  Group 2 (500 Hz–5 kHz):   C_apf = 300 pF
  Group 3 (2 kHz–20 kHz):  C_apf = 68–82 pF

Feedback transfer function (g = feedback gain, 0 ≤ g < 1):
  V_fb_source = (1 − FB_DIST_BLEND) × V_apf_out + FB_DIST_BLEND × V_post_dist
  V_out = H_6(s) × V_in / (1 − g × POLARITY × H_6(s))
  At g → 0.95: resonance approaching self-oscillation at notch frequencies

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • Expo converter matching: THAT340 or LM394 matched pair required for stable 1V/oct tracking across temperature. A single general-purpose transistor will drift. Calibrate at room temperature; verify at temperature extremes.
  • HF oscillation risk: at high I_abc (high frequency), OTA stages can self-oscillate due to parasitic capacitance. Add 22 pF capacitors at each OTA output node and keep traces short.
  • FB DIST BLEND circuit: continuous complementary resistive crossfade + op-amp buffer. No CD4053 needed. Buffer the BLEND_AMP output before the per-group feedback summing amps to isolate the crossfade pot loading from downstream.
  • POLARITY Off position: must connect cleanly to GND — a leaky switch contact causes audible bleed-through. Use a low-leakage panel switch or add a bleeder resistor to GND at the switch output.
  • V_post_dist routing: distortion output (Block 4) must be routed to APF feedback without picking up noise. Use shielded wire or dedicated PCB trace with guard ring; keep away from OTA signal nodes.
  • Crosstalk L/R: route L and R ground planes separately; do not route L signal adjacent to R signal on PCB.
  • PCB count: 18× LM13700 + 18× TL072 is large. Plan for 2–3 stacked PCBs with ribbon cable interconnect.
