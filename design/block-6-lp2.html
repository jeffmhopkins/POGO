4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

A second 2-pole lowpass filter stage identical in topology to LP1 but with completely independent controls and modulation. Placed in series after LP1, it extends the tone-shaping capability:

  • Stacked for steeper rolloff: with both cutoffs at the same frequency, approximately 24 dB/octave.
  • Layered for complex response: LP1 at low cutoff + LP2 at high cutoff creates a bandpass-like effect with resonant peaks at both frequencies.
  • Independent modulation: LP1 cutoff driven by envelope follower; LP2 cutoff driven by LFO or separate CV — creates independent sweeping on two stages.

Phase 2 — Transfer Function

H_LP2(s) = ω₀_2² / (s² + (ω₀_2 / Q_2) × s + ω₀_2²)

Combined LP1 + LP2 in series:
  H_combined(s) = H_LP1(s) × H_LP2(s)

When ω₀_1 = ω₀_2 and Q_1 = Q_2:
  Slope: 24 dB/octave; combined resonance peak: additive (much larger than single stage)

When ω₀_1 ≠ ω₀_2:
  Two separate knee points; bandpass-like region between cutoffs.

f_ref2 ≈ 2.5 kHz (set ~2 octaves above f_ref1 = 630 Hz so LP2 starts above LP1 by default)

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • Independent expo calibration: LP1 and LP2 expo converters must be calibrated separately. A CV that tracks perfectly on LP1 may be off on LP2 due to component tolerances. Always calibrate each stage independently.
  • Q formula and Iabc direction: Q = 52 mV / (Iabc × R_in). More Iabc → more damping → lower Q. Self-oscillation at Iabc → 0. LP2 RESONANCE control must decrease Iabc via its own IRES_AMP inverting driver (same circuit as LP1). RV_LP2_QMAX trims V_bias.
  • IC_Q_AB cell pairing: LP2 Q uses cell B of IC_Q_AB; LP1 Q uses cell A. Keep Iabc drive traces separate to avoid crosstalk between the two resonance controls.
  • Ground routing: use a single ground wire between LP1 and LP2 sections of the PCB; do not rely on copper pours alone between sections carrying different signal voltages.
  • All other circuit challenges are identical to LP1 — see Block 5.
