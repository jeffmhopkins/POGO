4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

A switchable gain boost placed right at the input, before any filtering or distortion. At unity, the signal passes through clean and transparent. At 5× (~14 dB), the input gain pushes quieter sources harder into the comb filter and distortion stages downstream, adding apparent saturation without changing filter parameters.

The toggle switch feels intentional and definitive — "signal" vs "driven." No soft ramping. Instantaneous gain change.

Phase 2 — Transfer Function

V_out = G × V_in

where G = 1.0  (toggle UNITY)
      G = 5.0  (toggle BOOST)

Non-inverting topology:  G = 1 + R_f / R_in

Unity:  G = 1  (direct feedback, R_f = 0, R_in = N/C — voltage follower)
Boost:  G = 5  →  R_f / R_in = 4  →  R_f = 40.2 kΩ, R_in = 10 kΩ

Saturation model (boost mode, TL072 on ±12 V supply):
  V_out = clamp(5 × V_in, −10.5 V, +10.5 V)
  Clipping onset: V_in ≥ ±2.1 V

Clipping is intentional at BOOST with hot inputs — the TL072 soft-clips slightly before the hard rail limit, adding gentle odd harmonics at onset. A clipped waveform feeding Block 2 (Envelope Follower) gives a very stable, predictable envelope CV — useful for drums or sustained notes.

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • Component values: R_f = 40.2 kΩ (E96 series, ≤0.5% error from 40 kΩ target), R_in = 10.0 kΩ (1%)
  • 2PDT toggle: one pole controls L feedback network, other pole controls R. Both switch simultaneously.
  • Switch wiring: toggle physically selects between wire (G=1) and R_f/R_in network (G=5). Use break-before-make or make-before-make — either acceptable since stages are not sensitive to momentary float during switching.
  • RF pickup: route toggle switch wires as short as possible to avoid RF pickup on the feedback node (high-impedance node).

Trim Pots

None required. Gain is set by 1% resistor ratios. Gain error <2% with 1% components.

Optional: if L/R gain matching better than 1 dB is needed, replace R_in per channel with 9.1 kΩ + 2 kΩ Bourns 3224W SMD trim pot in series.

Power

+12 V: ~3 mA  |  −12 V: ~3 mA
