4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

A voltage-controlled amplifier placed immediately before LP Filter 1, after the distortion summing stage. It functions as a dynamics gate — using the mod bus (typically driven by the envelope follower) to accent, duck, or swell the signal entering the filters.

  • Accent mode: AMT full CW, mod bus riding an envelope → filter feed rises with each transient, opening the filters dynamically.
  • Duck/gate mode: AMT full CCW (inverted) → louder input causes the filter feed to dip, creating a sidechain-duck or gating effect.
  • At default: AMT = center (0), mod bus at rest → signal passes at unity gain, VCA has no effect.

The AMT knob is the panel attenuverter for the VCA Level modulation destination. It controls both the depth and polarity of CV modulation. No separate level or gain control exists — the VCA only modulates; at rest (center) it is transparent.

Phase 2 — Transfer Function

Linear VCA — output is the product of input signal and control voltage:

  V_out = V_in × G_vca

  G_vca = clamp(AMT_gain × V_cv_in / 10, 0, 1)

  AMT_gain ∈ [−1, +1]   (bipolar attenuverter; center = 0)
  V_cv_in ∈ [0, 10 V]   (mod bus or override CV)

  At AMT center (0 V out): THAT 2180 GAIN pin biased for G = 1.0 (unity).
  Full CW AMT + 10V CV: GAIN at unity-gain current → G = 1.0 (full pass-through).
  Full CW AMT + 0V CV: GAIN driven toward minimum → G ≈ 0 (muted).
  Full CCW AMT + 10V CV: inverted → GAIN driven toward minimum → ducking.

THAT 2180 exponential law note:
  THAT 2180 uses exponential gain-in-dB law (current-controlled). For musical accent use,
  the dB law matches logarithmic loudness perception naturally.
  R_gain converts V_cv_att to the appropriate GAIN pin current.

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • THAT 2180 GAIN pin / R_gain: R_gain = 15 kΩ nominal starting value. Verify the exact unity-gain control current from THAT 2180 datasheet and recalculate R_gain if needed. RV_VCA_UNITY_L/R (Bourns 3224W 500 Ω, one per audio board) in series with R_gain gives ±3% trim range. Adjustment: AMT to center, CV IN unplugged, trim until output = input level (0 dB).
  • No IC sharing: THAT_VCA_L and THAT_VCA_R are separate ICs, one per audio board. No crosstalk risk from shared IC, no constraint on board placement relative to LP1.
  • Hot input headroom: Block 4 output can reach ±10 V. THAT 2180 handles signals well within ±12 V rails — safe at this level.
  • Tip-switching normalling: the CV IN jack normalizes mod bus to the VCA control path. Verify Thonkiconn PJ301M-12 sleeve-switching lug is connected to the mod bus rail; inserting a cable breaks this and routes the jack tip to the attenuverter instead.
  • Sourcing: THAT 2180 available from THAT Corporation directly and via Mouser. Standard distributor — no specialty Eurorack supplier required.

Power

+12 V: ~5 mA  |  −12 V: ~5 mA  (2× THAT 2180, one per audio board)
