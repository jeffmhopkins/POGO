Block VCA: Pre-LP1 Voltage-Controlled Amplifier

Phase 1 ✅   Phase 2 ✅   Phase 3 ✅   Phase 4 [ ]
Positioned between Block 4 (Distortion) and Block 5 (LP Filter 1). Shares V2164 IC with LP1: cells 1+2 for LP1 resonance Q; cells 3+4 for VCA L+R signal path. Both blocks must be on the same PCB.

4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

A voltage-controlled amplifier placed immediately before LP Filter 1, after the distortion summing stage. It functions as a dynamics gate — using the mod bus (typically driven by the envelope follower) to accent, duck, or swell the signal entering the filters.

  • Accent mode: AMT full CW, mod bus riding an envelope → filter feed rises with each transient, opening the filters dynamically.
  • Duck/gate mode: AMT full CCW (inverted) → louder input causes the filter feed to dip, creating a sidechain-duck or gating effect.
  • At default: AMT = center (0), mod bus at rest → signal passes at unity gain, VCA has no effect.

The AMT knob is the panel attenuverter for the VCA Level modulation destination. It controls both the depth and polarity of CV modulation. No separate level or gain control exists — the VCA only modulates; at rest (center) it is transparent.

Phase 2 — Transfer Function

Linear VCA — output is the product of input signal and control voltage:

  V_out = V_in × G_vca

  G_vca = clamp(AMT_gain × V_cv_in / 10, 0, 1)

  AMT_gain ∈ [−1, +1]   (bipolar attenuverter; center = 0)
  V_cv_in ∈ [0, 10 V]   (mod bus or override CV)

  AMT_gain = 0 (center detent):
    G_vca = 0 regardless of CV → but wait: at center AMT, mod bus has no effect,
    so V_cv_att = 0 V → G_vca = 0/10 = 0. This would mute the signal.

  Correction: the V2164 is biased so that at 0V control input, gain = 1 (unity).
  The AMT attenuverter at center (0V output) leaves the V2164 at its unity-gain bias point.
  Full CW AMT: 10V CV → maximum V2164 gain (limited to unity; G=1)
  Full CCW AMT: 10V CV → inverted to −10V, driving V2164 toward minimum gain (G→0)

  For ducking: V_cv_att = AMT_gain × V_cv_in (negative when AMT_gain < 0)
    High CV + inverted AMT → low gain → signal dips with loud input = ducking.

V2164 exponential law note:
  V2164 uses exponential gain law. For musical accent use, the exponential law is appropriate
  (matches logarithmic loudness perception). For linear amplitude modulation, a log-law pre-driver
  can be added, but is not required for the intended musical use case.

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • V2164 cell allocation: sharing V2164 with LP1 saves PCB space and cost but means both blocks must be on the same PCB. Verify that LP1 resonance control voltage and VCA control voltage do not crosstalk inside the V2164 package (cells are independent by design — keep control traces separated on PCB).
  • Unity gain at center detent: at AMT center, attenuverter output = 0 V → V2164 control input at 0 V → G = 1 (unity) at the V2164 internal reference. Verify with V2164 datasheet control law at 0 V ctrl; may require a small DC bias offset on the control input.
  • Exponential vs linear perception: for gentle accent modulation, the V2164 exponential law is helpful. For hard gating (modulation to silence), verify the V2164 reaches G ≈ 0 within the attenuverter's output swing range.
  • Hot input headroom: Block 4 distortion output can reach ±7.5 V at maximum drive. V2164 signal inputs are rated for signals within ±supply (±12 V here) — hot input is within safe range.
  • Tip-switching normalling: the CV IN jack normalizes mod bus to the VCA control path. Verify Thonkiconn PJ301M-12 sleeve-switching lug is connected to the mod bus rail; inserting a cable breaks this connection and routes the jack tip to the attenuverter instead.
  • Trim pots: none required. V2164 gain at 0 V control is inherently near unity. If a precise unity-gain offset is needed, a small fixed resistor or trim in the control path can compensate, but is typically not necessary.

Power

+12 V: ~2 mA  |  −12 V: ~2 mA  (incremental; V2164 IC shared with LP1)
