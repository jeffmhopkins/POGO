4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

Derives CV envelopes from the post-gain / pre-comb audio signal. Two independent followers — one per channel — each producing a 0–10 V CV that tracks the amplitude of the audio in real time. These envelopes are the primary modulation source for the entire module, creating filter sweeps, distortion changes, and formant shifts that are tightly coupled to the dynamics of the input.

  • Fast attack / fast release: percussive, tight CV that punches with every transient.
  • Slow attack / slow release: smooth, sustained CV that swells with the overall level.

Phase 2 — Analog Behavior Model

Stage 1: Full-wave precision rectifier
  V_rect = |V_in|

Stage 2: Peak detector with asymmetric RC
  Rising edge  (V_rect > V_peak): τ_attack  = R_atk × C_hold
  Falling edge (V_rect < V_peak): τ_release = R_rel × C_hold

  C_hold = 2.2 µF (film)
  Attack range:  τ = 0.1 ms (pot minimum) → 200 ms (pot maximum)
    R_atk_min ≈ 100 Ω (end-stop)  → τ = 100Ω × 2.2µF ≈ 0.22 ms
    R_atk_max ≈ 100 kΩ (pot + end) → τ = 100kΩ × 2.2µF ≈ 220 ms
  Release range: τ = 5 ms → 2 s
    R_rel_min ≈ 2.4 kΩ (end-stop)  → τ = 2.4kΩ × 2.2µF ≈ 5.3 ms
    R_rel_max ≈ 1 MΩ (pot + end)   → τ = 1MΩ × 2.2µF ≈ 2.2 s

Stage 3: Output scaling amplifier
  V_env = 2 × V_peak   (G = 2 non-inverting)
  Full-scale input ±5 V → V_peak = 5 V → V_env = 10 V
  Output clamped to 0–10 V via 10 V Zener

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • C_hold package: primary option is 2.2 µF X7R MLCC 0805 — acceptable leakage and DA for audio-rate envelope hold. Alternative: 2.2 µF polyester film TH (5mm pitch) for lower DA. PCB footprint should accommodate both to defer decision to bring-up.
  • Log-taper pots: standard log pots concentrate most of the rotation range at the bottom decade. If attack feel is too cramped at slow times, use a linear pot with an RC shaping network for pseudo-log response.
  • Diode leakage: at room temperature, 1N4148 leakage is negligible (~1–5 nA). At slow release settings with 2.2 µF, hold time is set by diode leakage. Acceptable for release times ≤ 2 s.
  • DC blocking (DNP): C_DC_BLK_L/R are 10 µF 0805 footprints bridged by 0Ω resistors on the PCB. Remove the bridge and populate the cap if DC offset from Block 1 causes non-zero ENV output at silence. Both footprints must be present on every board revision.
  • MOD SOURCE SEL — three positions:
    • L: ENV_L routed directly. Best for mono-left input or when only the left channel matters.
    • max(L,R): BAT54 diode-OR of ENV_L and ENV_R, buffered by EF2_D. Tracks whichever channel is louder at any instant — catches transients from either side. BAT54 Vf ≈ 0.15 V; negligible relative to 0–10 V range.
    • avg(L,R): Two equal 20 kΩ resistors from ENV_L and ENV_R into the high-impedance (+in) of EF1_D, producing (ENV_L + ENV_R) / 2. Best for balanced stereo material where a smooth, stable CV is preferred over peak-tracking.
    The selected signal arrives at the sleeve switching lug of the PRIMARY MOD SOURCE jack (tip-switching Thonkiconn). Inserting a cable disconnects the normalled ENV signal.
