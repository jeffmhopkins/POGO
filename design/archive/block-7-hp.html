4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

A 2-pole voltage-controlled highpass filter as the final filter stage. Applied after both LP stages, it sculpts low-frequency content: removing sub-bass rumble, adding tightness to the low end, or — at high resonance — creating a resonant emphasis at the HP cutoff that produces an aggressive, formant-like peak at the bottom of the frequency spectrum.

  • Low resonance: clean, transparent high-pass tilt.
  • High resonance: pronounced resonant peak; sweeping the cutoff with CV creates a "bubble" or "quack" effect.
  • Maximum resonance: self-oscillation at the HP cutoff frequency.
  • Range: 20 Hz to ~5 kHz (HP is primarily for low-end sculpting).

Phase 2 — Transfer Function

H_HP(s) = s² / (s² + (ω₀/Q) × s + ω₀²)

SVF simultaneously provides:
  H_LP(s) = ω₀² / (s² + ω₀/Q × s + ω₀²)   ← aux jack
  H_BP(s) = (ω₀/Q)×s / (s² + ω₀/Q × s + ω₀²) ← aux jack
  H_HP(s) = s² / (s² + ω₀/Q × s + ω₀²)     ← main output

  At s → 0 (DC): H_HP → 0 (blocks low frequencies)
  At s → ∞: H_HP → 1 (passes high frequencies)
  At resonance: |H_HP(jω₀)| = Q (peak height)

Integrator cap values:
  Target f₀ nominal = 1 kHz; I_abc_nominal = 10 µA
  g_m = 10µA / (2 × 26mV) = 192 µS
  C = g_m / ω₀ = 192µS / (2π × 1000) = 30.6 nF → use 33 nF (C0G)

Range: 20 Hz – 5 kHz (≈8 octaves; controlled by expo converter)

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • SVF summing amp polarity (RESOLVED): the SUM_AMP inverts. HP_SUM_L/R (TL072) half B = HP output buffer — configure as G = −1 inverting unity-gain (R_in = R_f, (+) = GND). This corrects the phase inversion at no extra IC cost. Annotate in schematic: "HP buffer configured as G = −1 to compensate SUM_AMP inversion."
  • OTA linearizing resistors: LM13700 has built-in linearizing diodes. Connect linearizing diode inputs to the OTA differential inputs via 1 kΩ series resistors for low distortion at high signal levels.
  • LM13700 Q Iabc sizing and direction: HP Q uses IC_Q_C cell A. Q = 52 mV / (Iabc × R_in) = 52 mV / (Iabc × 100 kΩ). More Iabc → more damping → lower Q. Self-oscillation at Iabc → 0. RESONANCE HP must decrease Iabc via IRES_AMP inverting driver (same circuit as LP1/LP2): V_Iabc = V_bias − V_RES_scaled, R_Iabc = 1 MΩ. RV_HP_QMAX trims V_bias.
  • Self-oscillation amplitude: at Q → ∞, oscillation amplitude limited by op-amp/OTA clipping. Output will be a slightly distorted sine. Acceptable behavior.
  • HP above LP1/LP2 cutoff: if HP cutoff is set above LP1/LP2 cutoff, audio is attenuated by both LP and HP; effective bandpass. Output level may be very low. Valid (if unusual) patch.
