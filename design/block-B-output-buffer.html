Signal levels:
  BAND OUT in:  ±5 V (up to ±8 V at high LP1 resonance peak)
  LEFT/RIGHT in: ±5 V (up to ±8 V at high HP resonance peak)
  Out: same levels, low-impedance (~1 kΩ series to jack)

1 kΩ series output resistor:
  - Limits current into short circuits: I_max = 12V / 1kΩ = 12 mA (TL072 handles ≤25 mA)
  - Prevents oscillation from capacitive cable loads
  - 1 kΩ + 100 pF cable cap: RC pole at 1.6 MHz — negligible in audio band
  - DC-coupled (no series capacitors — Eurorack convention)

4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

Transparent, low-impedance output buffers. Drive the output jacks and any downstream module without coloration, without frequency-dependent loading, and without the last filter stage's output impedance interacting with cable capacitance. Identical to Block A in philosophy — the signal should not change passing through this block.

BAND OUT (LP1 L / LP1 R): taps the LP1 output, before LP2 and HP filtering. Useful for parallel routing — send the LP1 output to reverb or delay while the full chain (LP2 + HP) goes to the mix. This is a mid-process tap: post-comb, post-distortion, post-VCA, post-LP1; pre-LP2 and pre-HP.

LEFT / RIGHT: main stereo output, tapped after the HP filter (end of the full signal chain).

Phase 2 — Transfer Function

H(s) = 1   (unity gain, all frequencies, flat phase response — same as Block A)
Flat from DC to ~3 MHz (TL072 GBW at unity gain).

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • 1 kΩ series placement: resistors go between op-amp output pin and jack tip — not after the jack. Placing them after the jack provides no protection from short circuits.
  • No series capacitors: output jacks are DC-coupled per Eurorack convention. Do not add capacitors in the audio output path.
  • Place decoupling caps within 1 mm of each TL072 supply pin.
  • TL072 not rail-to-rail: output swings to ~±10.5 V on ±12 V supply. Keep input signal ≤ ±10 V.
  • BAND OUT tap point: taken at LP1 output buffer output (post-LP1, before LP2). The same node feeds both the LP2 input and the BAND OUT buffer. No loading conflict — both loads see the LP1 buffer output through separate 1 kΩ series resistors.

Trim Pots

None required. Unity gain is set by direct feedback topology.

Power

+12 V: ~4 mA  |  −12 V: ~4 mA  (2× TL072, ~2 mA each)
