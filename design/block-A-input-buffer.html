4. Design Notes

Phase 1 — Sonic Intent

Transparent, unity-gain buffering. This block is invisible to the audio signal — its sole job is to isolate POGO's internal circuitry from whatever is plugged into the jacks, and to protect ICs from out-of-range voltages. No coloration. No filtering.

Phase 2 — Transfer Function

H(s) = 1   (unity gain, all frequencies, flat phase response)
V_out = V_in   (voltage follower: non-inverting, gain = 1)

Flat from DC to ~100 kHz. Op-amp output swings to within ~1.5 V of rails (TL072: ±10.5 V typical on ±12 V supply). Input offset voltage: ~3 mV typical (TL072) — negligible DC offset at output.

Phase 3 — Circuit Notes

  • 100 Ω series resistor: limits short-circuit current; protects source module from capacitive loading.
  • BAT54S clamp diodes: clamp to ±12 V rails. Polarity-sensitive — verify cathode/anode orientation in layout.
  • Voltage follower topology: output tied directly to inverting input (pin 2 or 6). No resistors needed — gain is exactly 1.
  • TL072 not rail-to-rail: keep internal signal levels ≤ ±10 V to avoid clipping. Eurorack audio at ±5 V is well within range.

Trim Pots

None required. Unity gain is set by direct feedback topology.

Power

+12 V: ~2 mA  |  −12 V: ~2 mA
